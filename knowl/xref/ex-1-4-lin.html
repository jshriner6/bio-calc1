Example 1.4.9. Linear Equations and Solutions.

The equation \(x+1 = 2x-1\) is a linear equation. A solution to this equation is \(x=2\) since \((2) + 1 = 2(2)-1\text{.}\) This solution could be found algebraically by solving for “\(x\)”:
\begin{align*} x+1 \amp= 2x -1\\ 1 \amp= x -1\\ 2 \amp= x \end{align*}
